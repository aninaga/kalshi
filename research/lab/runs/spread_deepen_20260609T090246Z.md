## Mechanism (stated BEFORE any numbers)

The live NBA spread/handicap ladder is set from the pregame line and **re-marks
slowly** because the handicap book is thinner and less-watched than the
moneyline. Once a game is live, the actual margin trajectory is the dominant new
information about the final margin. The anchoring claim: the implied home margin
`mid` **lags the live margin pace** ‚Äî when the margin pace-projection
`proj = margin¬∑2880/elapsed` is more extreme than the implied margin, the line is
"too low" on the leading side and the final margin finishes in the projected
direction more often than the line implies. Prior work
(`SPREADS_FINDINGS.md`) established this is NOT a pure 0.50-fill artifact (real
fill mid ‚âà 0.519, leaving +5.22c genuine residual gross), netting ‚âà **+1.9c/ct**
as a taker but **failing the gate** (CI lo < 0 at any nonzero spread; season/parity
unstable). My deepening question: can the edge be sharpened ‚Äî by conditioning on
where the anchoring mechanism is strongest (entry timing, gap magnitude, the
line genuinely disagreeing with pace) ‚Äî into a cost-robust, gate-PASSING edge?

## Bug-hunt (the result that decides the verdict)

Per the non-negotiables I decomposed the "improvement" rather than bank it:

1. **The edge ‚âà the estimator-bias term, everywhere.** Net c/ct tracks
   `(win_rate ‚àí avg_fill_price)¬∑100` almost exactly (me=2160: net +12.21c,
   bias +0.156). Late-entry "deepening" simply walks into games where the leader
   is winning ~60‚Äì72% but the at-the-money strike still prices ~0.53‚Äì0.56. The
   gate's `estimator_bias_vs_unconditional` adversarial **FAILS** at every
   late-entry config (|gap| 0.069‚Äì0.156 > 0.05 tolerance). This is the
   favorite-longshot / late-decided-game bias ‚Äî a different lane ‚Äî not anchoring.

2. **Null test kills the pace signal's contribution.** Replacing the pace-projection
   side with "bet whoever is currently AHEAD" (margin sign, ignoring pace) at
   me=1440 gives TRAIN net **+2.25c, win 0.595** ‚Äî essentially the same as the
   "anchoring" continuation (+3.54c). The pace projection adds ~nothing over
   "bet the team that's ahead."

3. **Continuation vs reversion is a mirror around the bias.** me=1080: continuation
   TRAIN +4.47 / reversion TRAIN ‚àí11.03 (‚âà ‚àíbias). A real directional alpha would
   not be a sign-flip of the same magnitude.

4. **Train‚Üíval instability / OOS inversion.** The point estimate is unstable in
   the deepening knob: me=1080 TRAIN +4.47 ‚Üí **VAL ‚àí1.59** (inverts); the clean
   anchoring filter inverts on val at me=600 (+2.91‚Üí‚àí1.40) and me=900 (+5.28‚Üí‚àí0.97).
   The only configs that stay positive on val are the deep late-game (me‚â•1200)
   regime ‚Äî i.e. the favorite-longshot bias, which fails est-bias by construction.
   Picking the surviving cell would be the `calib_alpha` mirage / p-hacking.

5. **Staleness ruled out.** At me=1440, tightening the freshness guard 8‚Üí0 min
   leaves net unchanged (3.5‚Äì3.8c): the edge is not a stale-line artifact.
