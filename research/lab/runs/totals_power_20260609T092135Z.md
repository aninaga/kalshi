## 3. Power / required-n estimate

Observed (realistic central fill, n=1,107): per-trade mean **μ = −0.00859**
(prob), per-trade std **σ = 0.4889** (the hold-to-settlement 0/1 variance
dominates; trades are one-per-game so ≈iid, little clustering inflation).
Per-trade SNR = μ/σ = **−0.0176**.

**Because μ < 0 at realistic cost, the bare CI lower bound `μ − 1.96σ/√n` is
negative for ALL n and there is NO finite n that closes it above 0.** This is the
decisive structural difference from spreads (whose μ = +0.0077 > 0 still admitted
a — unattainable — required n). For totals as a taker, more games never help: the
edge is on the wrong side of zero. Min detectable edge AT n=1,107 (bare 95% CI
half-width): **±2.84¢** — the season can only certify an edge ≳2.8¢ net, and the
realistic net is −0.86¢.

To give the most generous still-honest power figure, the **zero-half-spread
(fee-only) variant** (the +0.53¢ case in TOTALS_REFINE; earn the spread, still pay
the unavoidable PM 2% taker) measures **net +0.68¢, σ = 0.4889**:

| variant (honest fills) | net ¢/ct | bare n (CI lo>0) | + season/parity sub-gates (~2×) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **realistic (1.5¢ spread + 2% fee)** | **−0.86** | **∞ (μ<0)** | **∞** |
| zero-half-spread, fee-only | +0.68 | ≈19,900 | ≈39,900 |

Even the fee-only required n (≈19,900 bare / ≈39,900 with the stability
sub-gates) is **~18–36× the entire nontest season (1,107)** and ~15–30× all 1,320
cached games. **No prior seasons exist** to add (Kalshi/PM NBA markets launched

## 4. Is totals efficient, data-limited, or tradeable?

**EFFICIENT (taker-killed), not data-limited.** Three distinct facts make this a
stronger "efficient" verdict than spreads' "data-limited":

1. The realistic NET edge is **negative** (−0.86¢), so n is not even the binding
   constraint — there is no positive edge for n to certify. (Spreads' +0.77¢ net
   was at least the right sign; totals is not.)
2. Even gross-of-half-spread the totals edge (+0.68¢) is **~5× smaller than the
   spread gross (+3.4¢)**, so the one untested lever that COULD save spreads —
   maker fills to dodge the half-spread — is **far weaker for totals**: it would
   recover only ~+0.7¢ here vs ~+3.4¢ there, and ~+0.7¢ against σ=0.489 is still
   ~20k games short.
3. The OOS season-half split shows the residual **decaying to negative**
   (H1 +1.48 → H2 −3.19), the textbook market-efficiency signature — the price is
   learning to embed the pace signal over the season.

This completes the cross-market map: **winner = efficient; spread =
real-but-data-limited (one season too small); total = EFFICIENT (the residual is
honest but already priced; the historical PASS was a 0.50-fill artifact).** The
totals continuation residual is the documented null DIAGNOSTIC_2/TOTALS_REFINE
predicted, now confirmed at maximal nontest n with a clean leakage audit and a
fair-strike check.
