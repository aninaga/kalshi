## Phase 1 — the feature: `xcity_realized_signal`

### Why the within-city version died and how this one fixes the timing

`sonnet_weather` EDA item 5 rejected the within-city cross-day signal as look-ahead: city
B's day-E event OPENS ~14:00 UTC on day E−1, but day-(E−1)'s high occurs ~21:00 UTC on
E−1 — 7 hours AFTER the day-E market opened — so "prev-day realized at entry 30 min after
open" reads the future. The fix is not to discard the information but to time-stamp it
honestly: the day-(E−1) realized high becomes DEFENSIBLY public only once A's local day
E−1 is over and A's market has closed, and the as-of join must hide it from every earlier
bar. That lands the signal ~40–45% into B's day-E quote window — still ~13–16 h before B's
day-E high occurs (~21:00 UTC E). This feature carries the CROSS-city version of that
fixed-timing signal.

### Definition

One row per B event (local day E), schema `(event_id, known_from_ts, value)`:

```
value(B, E)     = mean over the other 4 cities A of  [ realized_A(E−1) − forecast_asof_A(E−1) − mu_A ]
known_from_ts   = max over the 4 contributors A of anchor_A(E−1)
anchor_A(D)     = max( last cached candle ts of A's day-D event + 60 s,
                       epoch(end of A's LOCAL calendar day D, IANA tz) + 3600 s )
```

- `realized_A` = settled YES-bucket representative from the cache pkl (`_bucket_bounds`);
  events without a unique YES bucket are **dropped, never guessed** (exactly one:
  `KXHIGHMIA-26APR11`).
- `forecast_asof_A` = last `forecast_high_f` parquet row with `known_from_ts <= anchor_A`
  (resolves to the lead-1 vintage; that field's own `known_from_ts` is conservative
  ISSUE-time, see `data_forecast_high_f_20260609T214420Z.md`).
- `mu_A` = **TRAIN-only** per-city mean surprise (debias): AUS 2.6914, CHI 1.7057,
  DEN 0.9006, MIA 2.0777, NYC 1.9977. Independent cross-check: these are exactly the
  negated `CITY_BIAS` constants fable_forecast froze from the same train population.
  The value encodes SURPRISE, not level.
- Timezones: NYC/MIA `America/New_York`, CHI/AUS `America/Chicago`, DEN `America/Denver`
  (DST-aware via `zoneinfo`).
- A row missing ANY of its 4 contributors (no cached event, no unique YES bucket, no
  forecast) is dropped. Cache pkl loads retry once (another lane rewrites candles
  atomically).

### Why `known_from_ts` is defensible (the conservative-anchor argument)

The cache does not store Kalshi's `close_time`, so I anchored on two facts that are both
derivable offline and individually sufficient to bound publication:

1. **The market window is over**: `last candle ts + 60 s`. Empirically the candle grid runs
   to local ~23:59 or ~00:59 next day (two close-time regimes in the history); a book that
   died early (min observed: −1.9 h before midnight) does NOT lower the anchor because of
   the max().
2. **A's local calendar day is over by ≥ 1 h**: `end of local day + 3600 s`. By then the
   daily high is physically fixed and publicly observable (real-time NWS obs; the closed
   Kalshi book itself).

Empirically the max() resolves to **01:00 local time of day D+1 for every one of the 1,250
cached events** (`anchor − local_midnight_end` ≡ +1.000 h). We never claim "approximately
when the high occurred". Residual caveat (documented in provenance): Kalshi's formal
settlement confirmation can postdate the anchor; the VALUE used is the deterministic
bucketization of a quantity (the day's high) that is fixed and public at the anchor.

### Aggregation choice (TRAIN EDA, frozen BEFORE the field was written)

Two pre-declared candidates, compared on TRAIN only (875 panels), in both correlation and
price space: **mean-of-4** vs **nearest-city** (NYC↔CHI, MIA→NYC, AUS↔DEN by great-circle).
Both showed `corr(value, final − mid@arrival) ≈ 0` (−0.0025 / +0.0082); mean-of-4 was frozen
as the registered aggregation (matches the shared-model-bias mechanism, more averaging, and
the request spec's lead example). Honest pre-scoring note, recorded in the hypothesis text
itself: TRAIN lag-1 cross-city surprise correlations are ~0 (±0.1) while the only real
structure in the matrix is WITHIN-city day-over-day persistence (diagonal +0.15…+0.24) —
out of scope for this lane. The prior was weak and the gate decided.

### Point-in-time hand-verification (matching the forecast lane's standard)

`--verify-pit` (re-runnable) does three things:

1. **Global timezone-leakage assertion, every row**: for all 1,241 rows × 4 contributors =
   **4,964 checks**, `known_from_ts >= contributor's local end-of-day + 3600 s` AND
   `>= contributor's last candle ts + 60 s`, with the contributor table re-derived from the
   raw cache (not from the writer's intermediates). All pass ⇒ no row's `known_from_ts`
   pre-dates a defensible publication time in ANY contributor's timezone.
2. **Join boundary, 3 hand events** (train×2, val×1): the joined panel series is NaN at
   every bar strictly before `known_from_ts` and exactly equals the stored value from the
   first bar at/after it; `known_from_ts` lies INSIDE B's quote window (the row is usable,
   not just unleaked):

   | event | known_from_ts (UTC) | value F | first visible bar | frac | NaN bars before |
   |---|---|---|---|---|---|
   | KXHIGHNY-26JAN15 | 2026-01-15 08:00 | +0.756 | 1019 | 0.447 | 1019 |
   | KXHIGHDEN-25NOV20 | 2025-11-20 07:00 | +1.357 | 870 | 0.400 | 870 |
   | KXHIGHAUS-26APR20 | 2026-04-20 07:00 | +0.755 | 988 | 0.425 | 988 |

   (08:00 UTC = 01:00 MST Denver — the max-anchor contributor for an NYC event in
   January; 07:00 UTC = 01:00 CST/CDT for the AUS/CHI-bound rows. Exactly the rule.)
3. **Independent value recompute** for the same 3 events from raw cache + forecast parquet
   (`mean of other-4 debiased surprises`) — matches the stored value to 1e-9.

Structurally, `feature_store._asof_series` (searchsorted-right) makes a future-issued row
unreachable by an earlier bar; the checks above verify the rule AND the implementation.

## Phase 2 — PRE-REGISTERED hypothesis `cc772c239008304b`

Registered + claimed 2026-06-09T23:33:58Z, before any gate scoring. Parameters below are
EXACTLY as registered; nothing retuned after seeing val/nontest.

- **Mechanism**: when the other cities came in WARM vs their debiased model forecasts on
  day E−1, city B's still-open day-E market underadjusts → buy "over"; symmetric
  cold/under.
- **Signal**: first bar `i` with `elapsed_frac ∈ [0.35, 0.70]`, `stale_min[i] ≤ 2`, finite
  mid & feature, `|xcity_realized_signal[i]| ≥ 1.0 F` (≈ 0.7σ of the feature —
  retention-motivated; TRAIN threshold response was non-monotone and this was recorded
  pre-scoring rather than peak-picked).
- **Direction**: over if value > 0 else under, at the ATM-snapped strike, hold to
  settlement.
- **Execution**: i+1 latency (1 min); freshness ≤ 2 min;
  `FillModel(venue="kalshi", half_spread=max(2×0.0067 train median, 0.015)=0.015)`;
  `min_elapsed=0, max_elapsed=200000` (weather override of the NBA default); one trade per
  event. **No pick_strike pinning needed**: the side conditions on the exogenous feature
  sign only, never on a quote level, so the i+1 ATM re-snap cannot invert exposure (the
  snap-flip hazard applies to quote-level-conditioned signals).

## Bug hunt

- **Timezone leakage (the lane's #1 hazard)**: no row's `known_from_ts` pre-dates any
  contributor's local end-of-day+1h or market-close — proven by 4,964 re-derived
  assertions over EVERY stored row (not a sample), plus the 3-event NaN-prefix boundary
  check on the joined panels. The eastern-A-vs-western-B same-day variant (NYC day-E
  realized vs DEN's last ~2 stale hours) was excluded by design — only day-(E−1)
  contributors are used.
- **Selection effects**: entries condition on an exogenous value and a time window, never
  on quote levels; estimator-bias check passes on both splits (fill ≈ unconditional 0.497;
  win-rate gap = claimed gross). The B event's own city never contributes to its row, and
  contributors are a different calendar day, so no settlement information can circulate
  into its own panel.
- **Debias honesty**: `mu_A` is TRAIN-only, frozen, documented in provenance, and
  cross-checked against fable_forecast's independently-computed constants (match to 0.02 F).
  Train rows are mildly in-sample w.r.t. the mean (standard train-EDA allowance);
  val/test-period rows use strictly historical constants.
- **Dropped-row honesty**: the one nontest trade with NaN payoff (`KXHIGHMIA-26APR11`, no
  unique YES bucket) is dropped by `evaluate` (399 → 398), not imputed.
- **Snap-flip**: side from exogenous sign at ATM ⇒ re-snap harmless (documented above).
- **Reproducibility**: two back-to-back nontest runs produce identical trades
  (game_ids, sides, pnl with `equal_nan`).
- **Test split**: never read. Splits respected via `data.load_panels('temp', split=...)`.

## Verdict & what the family record gains

**DEAD.** Cross-city day-(E−1) realized-vs-forecast surprise carries no tradeable
information for the next day's other-city markets: the TRAIN cross-correlation structure

cost floor, the CI straddles zero on both splits, and both OOS halves are negative. This
is the 8th weather-family mechanism falsified and the 2nd exogenous-information one (after
the forecast-gap −0.08c): the family pattern — gross inefficiencies live at/below the
taker cost floor — now extends to cross-event information flow.

hand-verified, provenance'd) now attaches to every temp panel for future lanes. The one
positive lead the EDA surfaced — WITHIN-city day-over-day surprise persistence (lag-1
autocorr +0.15…+0.24), which is point-in-time tradeable with this same 01:00-local anchor —
belongs to a different lane/hypothesis and is left on the table, explicitly NOT claimed
