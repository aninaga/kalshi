## What was built

   glue (`_live_weather_events` / `_live_weather_rec` / `_build_live_panel`)
   is now one entry in a per-family hook registry `LIVE_FAMILIES`
   (`events` / `rec` / `panel` / `maker_book`), joined by a crypto entry
   built on the crypto provider's fetch helpers (`_kalshi_fetch` +
   `providers.crypto._boundary/_iso_ts/event_date_hour`). Open passes
   resolve hooks from the tenant spec's `family`; `--mark`/`--settle`
   resolve them from each position ROW's recorded `family`, so one book
   could hold mixed families and settlement always uses the right panel
   builder. Network stays in the CLI-path hook implementations only; every
   hook is injectable in tests. Existing seam parameters
   (`live_events`/`live_rec`/`live_panel`) keep working and override hooks.
   - Crypto live glue specifics: `_live_crypto_rec` fetches the top-24
     thresholds by cumulative volume among volume>0 markets (the cache

     settle passes, and captures `close_ts` + `expiration_value`.
     `_build_live_crypto_panel` applies the same live-truncation
     `duration_sec` fix the weather builder got, + `feature_store.join_panel`.

## Caveats / honest notes

- Both books are MEASURE-ONLY: they record and settle, and their factory
  docstrings forbid promotion claims that do not independently satisfy the
  AGENDA hard rules (>= 12 distinct holdout settlement events AND declared-K
  alpha/K correction). The BTC pilot's declared K = 386 travels with it.
- The BTC entry-minute quote is the LAST two-sided candle close of the
  trailing fetch, and the recorded fill is at that minute's displayed ask —
  the memos' cross-through convention (their holdout cross PnL was
  +1.15..+1.38c). With the real 1c-ceil taker fee, 99c-ask signals fail the
  frozen cost line by construction, so recorded entries effectively span
  asks of 95-98c.
- Top-24-by-volume rec fetch can in principle drop a qualifying threshold
  ranked below 24; accepted for rate-limit hygiene and documented in the
  hook docstring.
- Expected signal volume is TINY by design: one hour-20 BTC event/day with a
  30-min entry band at a 15-min cadence; the fillprobe inherits v1's signal
  rate (~5 events/day across 5 cities) cut to 2 cities x a 10-hour local
  window. Weeks, not days, to a verdict — that is the pilot-tiny intent.
