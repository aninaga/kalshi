## EDA observation that motivated it (train only, idea-agnostic)

Boundary-level calibration on the 875 train panels, sampling every ladder
quote at window fractions {0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 0.9} and bucketing by price,
shows the classic favorite-longshot signature at every fraction:

| price bucket | gap (realized − quoted), by fraction |
|---|---|
| p < 0.05 | −1.6 to −1.8¢ (longshots overpriced) |
| p in [0.05, 0.10) | −2.4 to −3.9¢ |
| p in [0.90, 0.95) | +1.9 to +5.3¢ (favorites underpriced) |
| p ≥ 0.95 | +0.6 to +1.5¢ |

Feasibility scan: 93% of train events have ≥1 fresh bar (stale ≤ 2 min,
≥1 h warm-up) where the favorite side of the mid-snapped boundary quotes in
[0.85, 0.97], median first hit at ~74% of the quote window — i.e.
mid-afternoon of the target day, after the daily high is typically realized,
exactly where lagging-toward-certainty quotes should live.

## Mechanism

Retail lottery demand plus lazy wide quoting on thin daily-high tails
overprice low-probability boundary outcomes; the FAVORITE side of the
ATM-snapped boundary is therefore systematically underpriced, especially
late in the window once the high is largely realized but quotes lag toward
certainty.

First fresh bar (stale ≤ 2 min, elapsed ≥ 3600 s) where the favorite-side

**buy_favorite_side** ("over" if P(over) ≥ 0.5 else "under"), hold to

## Bug-hunt: the snap-flip inversion (found, fixed BEFORE final scoring)

First train run (n=765): avg fill **0.711** against a registered band of
[0.85, 0.95] — impossible if the executed trade matched the hypothesis.
Cause: `FillModel` re-snaps to the strike nearest `mid` at the **i+1 fill
timestamp**. When mid sits near the midpoint of two boundaries, a ≤0.05°F
one-minute move flips the snap, and the side label executes against the
OTHER strike — e.g. KXHIGHAUS-25DEC05: signal snap 55.5 (favorite = under @
0.85); at fill the snap flipped to 53.5 where P(over)=0.87, so "under"
filled at **0.13** — a longshot buy, the exact opposite exposure. **22.6%**
of unguarded fills landed >10¢ below the signal-bar favorite quote.
Unguarded train result for the record: −1.50¢/ct, CI [−3.65, +0.53], n=765.

Fix (execution integrity, not signal tuning; ratio fixed at 0.5 when the bug
was found, before re-scoring): only fire when mid is decisively nearest one
strike — `dist(nearest) ≤ 0.5 × dist(second nearest)`, bar-i info only (no
look-ahead). Post-guard fills match the registered trade (avg fill 0.852 in
band; estimator-bias check passes: win rate 0.862 vs price 0.852).

## Why it's dead (the economics)

The calibration gap in the EDA is real but lives in the deep tails
(p < 0.10 / p > 0.95) of NON-ATM boundaries late in the window — quotes the
substrate's ATM-snap execution cannot reach, and where mid is clipped/stale
so the freshness guard correctly blocks. The *executable* slice of the bias
(the snapped boundary in [0.85, 0.95] with a stable snap) carries only ~1¢
gross — under half the ~2.6¢ all-in taker cost. A maker-style fill could in
principle flip the sign, but that is an execution study, not this
hypothesis, and is not claimed.
