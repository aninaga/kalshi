## Phase 1 — TRAIN-only EDA (724 splits ids → 723 usable panels)

Coverage: 723 train panels, ALL ~1h/60-bar windows (BTC stride-2 lands on
even hours, so the ~25h 17:00-EDT daily book is absent from this cache —
window-heterogeneity caution noted but moot for this run; entry still
conditions on time-to-close, not elapsed fraction). 581 BTC / 142 ETH;
median ladder 24 strikes; measured per-minute half-spread median 0.61c,
p90 2.0c; every panel carries the exact `expiration_value` settle.

Three candidate classes probed (bar-level, fresh quotes `stale_min<=2` only):

1. **Mid-calibration drift by time-to-close** — calibration of the
   ATM-snapped strike's quoted prob `p` vs outcome `y` (`final > strike`),
   pooled bar-rows: mean(p−y) = −1.7c at ttc 45–60 min, ~0c at 10–30 min,
   **+2.2c at 6–10, +3.3c at 3–6, +2.4c at 0–3** — a monotone late-window
   sell-side premium on the nearest-to-money threshold. Per-event (one obs at
   the first fresh bar in the window — the strategy's shape):
   ttc[2,10) +0.95c ± 1.52c se; **ttc[2,6) +3.09c ± 1.36c se (n=708)**;
   ttc[2,4) +4.28c ± 1.22c. Sign consistent BTC (+2.4c)/ETH (+3.6c) and
   2026-04 (+2.2c)/2026-05 (+3.2c) at the bar level; hour-of-day clusters
   noisy at n≈49/cluster (BTCH16 −16c … BTCH18 +12c) — flagged for the gate's
   cluster knockout to arbitrate, not hand-filtered.
2. **Drift-fade** (fade the last-10-min implied-price move at the re-snapped
   ATM): per-event +4.3c ± 1.85c at |drift|≥20bps (n=487) but threshold-
   sensitive (−0.6c at 10bps) and two extra parameters. The naive
   corr(drift, future-move) = −0.7 is mostly MECHANICAL (both terms share
   `mid[i]`) and was discounted.
3. **Staleness-reaction**: big conditional deviations (mean(y−p) −31c at
   stale≥6 min, n=213) but UNTRADEABLE — you cannot fill against a frozen
   book at its stale quote; rejected on execution honesty, not on effect size.

**Pick: #1** — simplest walking skeleton (no engineered feature, no
quote-level side condition, one window parameter pair), largest clean n.

## Phase 2 — pre-registration (BEFORE any strategy PnL)

Registered `f0dcfdc43c86fd58`:

* **Mechanism**: deadline compression / longshot premium — as the hourly
  settlement approaches, the remaining-move distribution narrows faster than
  the quoted ATM threshold converges; the just-OTM "$X or above" lottery leg
  keeps too much mass on a late cross; sellers collect.
* **Signal**: first bar with time-to-close in **[2, 6) minutes**, quote fresh
  (mid stale ≤ 2 min), finite implied mid.
* **Direction**: SELL (`side="under"`, unconditional) the ATM-snapped
  threshold at i+1 (1-min latency; ATM re-snap at fill is correct for an ATM
  strategy and the unconditional side cannot be inverted by a snap flip),
  hold to settlement.
* **Frozen params**: `TTC_LO_MIN=2.0`, `TTC_HI_MIN=6.0`,
  `entry_latency_min=1.0`, `max_stale_min=2.0`, exit=settlement,
  `FillModel(venue="kalshi", half_spread=0.015)` where 0.015 =
  max(2 × 0.0061 measured TRAIN median, 0.015) — the xcity convention.

No new feature was built → no `--verify-pit` phase (entry reads only panel
quotes and the clock; PIT-safety is the provider's, already tested).

## Verdict — **DEAD**

The premium is REAL gross (+2.7c train / +2.3c nontest, sign-stable across
splits, assets, and months — the EDA was not a mirage) but it is an
execution-cost-sized premium, not an edge: the 1.5c pre-registered
half-spread plus the ~1.7c Kalshi taker fee at a ~0.58 fill consume it
exactly. Net is negative on BOTH splits with bootstrap CIs spanning zero.
Settled `verdict="DEAD"`, `status="done"` in the registry; ledger row
appended (family=crypto, market=coin_price).

What survives the death:

* The family's e2e seam is proven: Panel → ATM snap → FillModel → settlement
  → gate all behaved; the estimator-bias and concentration checks passed,
  i.e. the loss is honest economics, not a harness artifact.
* The gross premium is a real, measured phenomenon (+3.3c/bar at ttc 3–6
  min). It is dead as a TAKER strategy at 1.5c half-spread + taker fee. A
  MAKER variant (collect the spread instead of paying it, fee schedule
  differs) is the obvious successor hypothesis — same signal, different
  execution primitive; cf. the maker-fill study lane.
* Hour-of-day cluster noise (BTCH16 −16c vs BTCH18 +12c in train EDA) never
  got large enough to trip the concentration gate but is worth a look if a
  successor revives the signal.
