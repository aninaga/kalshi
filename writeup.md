Substitution and foul-trouble events create temporary pricing dislocations because Polymarket makers and flow tend to incorporate lineup changes only after several bars, while implied home win probability can remain pinned to stale momentum assumptions; when the trailing team suddenly posts a clear star advantage on floor, the trailing side’s true win chance is repriced upward faster in the game than in the market. By buying the trailing team only when `pm_implied_wp` is already in a deep discount (<=0.20 when home is trailing, >=0.80 when away is trailing) and star counts strongly favor the underdog side, we trade exactly the window where the book is most likely to be behind the true state transition. This should produce a mean-reversion-like correction in contract value as bettors reprice the lineup change, with a more durable signal than pure momentum because it is anchored to a discrete lineup feature and a hard price-level misalignment. Expected gross edge is in the low-single-cent regime, around +2 to +4 cents per contract before costs, with moderate confidence because these events are intermittent and sometimes reflected quickly. The primary failure mode is that the edge is regime dependent: if substitutions happen when expected points remain unchanged (defensive possessions only, garbage time, or with both teams already fully priced in), the market may not move enough and slippage from realistic costs can erase the edge. It is also vulnerable to narrow sample noise because star-on-floor changes are sparse and concentrated in certain teams and game scripts, so sample quality rather than signal shape may dominate out-of-sample performance. The bracketed profit/loss exits are meant to protect against exactly those overfitting cases while preserving upside when repricing is delayed.
